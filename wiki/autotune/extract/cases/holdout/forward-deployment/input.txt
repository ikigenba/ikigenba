Forward deployment is a release practice in which every merged change ships to production automatically and is corrected by a follow-up change rather than a rollback. The practice was popularized by the engineer Sana Whitfield in a widely cited 2017 talk. Forward deployment is common at large software platforms. Detractors say it demands unusually strong monitoring, though adoption continues to grow.
