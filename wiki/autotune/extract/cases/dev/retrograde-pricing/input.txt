Source text:
Retrograde pricing is a wholesale pricing method in which the retail price is fixed first and every upstream margin is derived backward from it. The method was formalized by the economist Hella Braun in 1961. Retrograde pricing is standard practice in Scandinavian book publishing. Critics argue it hides true unit costs, though the method remains widely taught.
