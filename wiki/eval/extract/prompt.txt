Extract subjects and claims from the source text.

Return JSON with shape {"subjects":[{"type":"entity|event|concept","kind":"...","name":"...","occurred_at":"...","claims":["..."]}]}.
Use only the source text and document header; do not infer unstated facts.

Choose subjects conservatively:
- Prefer the named people, organizations, products, places, or other entities that the header or main sentences are about.
- Emit a secondary named thing only when the text gives it independent factual claims, not merely because it is a transaction, date-bearing action, facility, division, model, technology, setting, or object connected to a primary subject.
- Treat acquisitions, openings, launches, appointments, partnerships, studies, releases, financial periods, and similar dated actions or reports as claims about the participating entities unless the occurrence itself has a proper name and is discussed independently.
- Treat concepts, methods, industries, technologies, features, metrics, and business segments as claim wording unless the source defines or discusses the concept itself.
- Avoid duplicate subjects for the same real-world thing; use one canonical name exactly as identified by the source.

Write claims as short, self-contained prose statements with no pronouns. Keep separately stated facts as separate claims rather than combining several facts into one sentence.
For each subject, include all directly stated attributes, roles, relationships, dates, actions, outcomes, ownership, affiliations, quantities, comparisons, and changes that are facts about that subject.

Perform a clause-by-clause coverage pass after choosing subjects:
- For every sentence, list its independent factual predicates, including predicates joined by and, while, but, or semicolons; numeric results; changes and comparisons; named features; dates; and appositive or possessive facts.
- Assign each predicate to every emitted subject that the clause explicitly makes its actor, holder, described entity, or changed party. Do not omit a predicate merely because another claim from the same sentence was already included.
- Include a claim when the subject is explicitly named or clearly referred to in a clause that states a fact about it, including appositive, possessive, passive, founder/creator, ownership, affiliation, or role phrases.
- A relationship may yield a claim for each emitted subject only when the source clause directly anchors that relationship to each subject, not merely because one subject is mentioned inside another subject's action.
- Preserve stable reciprocal facts for emitted subjects when directly stated, especially origin, founding, creation, authorship, ownership, affiliation, identity, and completed transactions.
- For future-tense facts involving two emitted subjects, assign the claim to the emitted subject that will act, decide, join, keep, control, appoint, retain, manage, or otherwise carry out the future action. Do not also make it a claim about the affected emitted subject unless that affected subject is itself the future actor or the clause states a present stable fact about it.
- Future facts about a facility, program, division, product, brand, or other thing that is not emitted as its own subject may be included under the owning emitted subject when the source directly states them.
- Do not add explanatory context, motives, or consequences unless the source states them as facts about that subject.

Keep claim wording lexically faithful:
- Reuse the source's principal verb and specific nouns wherever possible, while replacing pronouns with the subject name. Preserve negation, modality, tense, quantities, units, periods, directions, and comparison baselines.
- Do not replace a specific predicate with a broader interpretation or summary. Do not add a second paraphrase of a predicate already captured.
- Split coordinated clauses only at independent predicates. Repeat a shared subject or reporting frame as needed to make each claim self-contained, but keep modifiers with the predicate they qualify.

Handle reported metrics with literal predicate boundaries:
- A metric's value, its change from another period, and an explicit comparison or cause are separate claims only when the source states each as a separate predicate. Preserve the named metric, value, units, period, direction, and comparison baseline in the applicable claim.
- Introductory reporting verbs such as "reported", "said", "announced", or "posted" do not create an extra claim when the substantive metric or result is already captured. Do not emit the metric, period, business segment, or quoted speaker as a subject merely because it appears in the report.
- Do not derive unstated values, trends, causes, rankings, or summaries from the numbers. When several numbers are only components of one stated total or result, keep them with that result instead of inventing additional predicates.

Keep predicate direction faithful to the source:
- Do not invent a causative or receiving frame for a subject by rewriting another subject's action as that subject having, getting, receiving, adding, bringing in, or being joined by someone or something.
- Use that kind of receiving or possession claim only when the source directly states the subject receives, gains, owns, appoints, hires, creates, or undergoes the change, or when a completed transaction directly changes that subject.
- If a faithful claim would require changing who performs the verb, omit it for that subject and keep the fact with the subject that the source makes the actor, holder, or changed party.

Normalize fields consistently:
- Use type "entity" for people, organizations, places, products, facilities, documents, and other concrete named things; use "event" only for an independently named occurrence; use "concept" only for an abstract idea that is itself discussed.
- Use a concise lowercase kind such as "person", "organization", "company", "product", "place", or "event" based only on what the source supports. Do not encode roles, industries, or descriptions in kind.
- occurred_at is required for events. For entities and concepts, omit occurred_at unless the source directly associates the subject itself with a date; never copy a document date into every subject.
- When occurred_at is present, it must be an ISO-8601 prefix (YYYY, YYYY-MM, or YYYY-MM-DD).

type must be one of "entity", "event", or "concept"; kind and name must be non-empty; each subject needs at least one non-empty claim.
