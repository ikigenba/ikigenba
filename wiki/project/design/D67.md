# Decision 67 — The improvement loop: one ralph turn per candidate, driven by autotune

**Decision.** Prompt tuning is an **unattended ralph loop** over the D64–D66 workbench, launched and supervised by the `autotune` driver (D68) — never launched bare. `eval/extract/improve.md` is the committed, single-prompt ralph loop body; each turn runs in a fresh context; all state lives in the untracked workspaces (D64). The driver owns bootstrap, stop budgets, and finalization; the prompt owns exactly one improvement turn. This decision is **structural**: it fixes the loop's protocol and the committed assets; every checkable behavior it leans on is owned by D64–D66 and D68.

The protocol `improve.md` encodes:

- **Precondition, not bootstrap** — the driver has already provisioned `tmp/autotune/extract/` (resolved `config.json`, `baseline.json` with epsilon, `best/`, the built `bin/eval-extract`) and seeded `autotune/extract/prompt.txt` before any turn runs. A turn that finds the workspace unprovisioned does **not** rebuild it: it reports the missing piece and declares `DONE` — fail loudly, never re-derive state the driver owns.
- **One turn = one candidate** — read `history.md` and the incumbent's scorecard (especially per-case misses and spurious claims); write **one** revised candidate under `candidates/NNN-prompt.txt`; `run` it against the **dev** split with `-repeat 3` and the **workspace** config (`tmp/autotune/extract/config.json` — never the committed one, so the driver's `-c` step-model choice governs every call); `compare` against `best/scorecard.json`; on accept (exit 0) promote it to `best/` **and copy it over `autotune/extract/prompt.txt`** (the visible incumbent the driver diffs, D64/D68); on reject keep the incumbent; append the history line either way.
- **Status protocol** — end the turn `CONTINUE` after a normal candidate, accepted or rejected: there is **no reject-streak stop**; a plateaued loop keeps proposing until the operator's budget or interrupt ends it (D68). Declare `DONE` only when the incumbent's dev mean composite is exactly **1.0** (nothing left to improve) or on the unprovisioned-workspace error above.
- **No finalization in the loop** — the turn never runs the holdout split, writes no summary, and never inspects holdout cases while proposing or judging; holdout-once, the summary, and the final diff are the driver's exit work (D68), so they happen even when ralph is killed mid-turn.
- **Hard boundaries, stated in the prompt and enforced outside it** — the loop writes **only** under `tmp/autotune/extract/` and `autotune/extract/` (both untracked, D64). It never edits `eval/extract/` (prompt, config, gold, improve.md itself), `internal/`, or `cmd/`. Enforcement is mechanical, not politeness: everything it could corrupt is committed, so `git status` after a run shows a clean tree, and the winner's score must **reproduce** — the operator re-runs `eval-extract run` on the claimed winner from the clean checkout before adopting; a score that doesn't reproduce (beyond epsilon) rejects the run.
- **Adoption is manual** — copying the winner over `eval/extract/prompt.txt` is an operator decision made through the normal spec/commit channel (D64), never something the loop or driver does.

`eval/extract/README.md` is the operator guide, rewritten for the driver era: launching `autotune`, choosing step and improver models, reading the workspaces and the printed diffs, the reproduce-then-adopt step, and how the gold corpus grows (operator-reviewed, D64).

**Rejected.**
- **Keeping bootstrap and finalization in the loop prompt** (the previous form of this decision) — bootstrap in the prompt duplicated driver work and made the first turn special; finalization in the prompt evaporated whenever a budget or Ctrl-C killed ralph before the closing turn. Both belong to the process that survives the loop.
- **The 5-consecutive-rejects auto-stop** (previous form) — contradicted the operator's "run until my budget says stop"; a reject streak is information for the improver, not a stop condition. Budgets, perfection, and interrupts are the only stops.
- **Accumulating one long improver context across turns** — each attempt should reason from scorecard evidence, not its own chat history of failed theories; fresh context per turn is the feature.
- **Letting the loop adopt winners into `eval/extract/prompt.txt`** — collapses the tune/adopt boundary the product promises; adoption stays a deliberate human change.
- **Running holdout every iteration** — quietly turns holdout into a second dev set and destroys its meaning as a generalization check.

**Verification.** Pure structure and protocol — no ids of its own. Its proof is the behavioral ids it composes: the pins and workspaces (R-KICZ-E6WA, R-KJKV-RYMZ, R-KKSS-5QDO), the scorer and acceptance arithmetic (D65's ids, especially R-KWZR-ZFSM and R-ESN9-RFM1), the runner's run/repeat/compare/exit/progress contracts (D66's ids), and the driver's lifecycle ids (D68). The committed assets themselves (`improve.md`, `README.md`, the gitignore lines) are checked structurally by their build phase.
