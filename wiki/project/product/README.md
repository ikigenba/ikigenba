# wiki — Product

**Authority: intent.** This document owns *why* the wiki exists, *for whom*, what is in and out of scope, and what we **promise** the user — in outcome terms only. Mechanism, data formats, schema, exit codes, and test assertions live in `project/design/README.md`. Where the two touch observable behavior, product states the *promise* and design states the *exact, checkable proof*; that boundary keeps product, design, and plan from overlapping.

## Problem

Knowledge worked out through an agent evaporates when the session ends, and the next conversation starts cold. Retrieval over raw chunks re-derives understanding every time — nothing accumulates, cross-references go undrawn, contradictions unreconciled — while a hand-maintained wiki collapses under its own upkeep. The user is left with no durable, compounding, inspectable store of what they've learned.

Running such a store surfaces more pains. The ingest is LLM-backed, and LLMs fail invisibly — most often by not returning the structured content the ingest needs — so failed and aborted work must be siftable, stoppable, and cleanly redoable, with each call's footprint attributed clearly enough (which stage, which job, which question) that a fix is not a guess; the suite keeps that record in one place, and wiki must label its calls so the record is usable. Each ingest is minutes of work, so if it holds the store while it runs, status checks, questions, and new ingests all stall — and one hung job wedges the whole service. And every LLM call site is a quality-and-cost choice (which model, thinking level, generation settings), so each must be tunable without moving the others. The extract prompt in particular is the quality ceiling of everything downstream — every subject, claim, and page inherits its errors — yet without measurement a prompt edit's effect is invisible, and improving it by eyeballing outputs is guesswork.

## Purpose

The wiki is ikigenba's knowledge base: an MCP service that *compiles* ingested text into durable per-subject pages and answers questions from them. Its primary surface is **MCP** (agents work through tools, not screens), but it also serves a small **human web surface** at its mount root: a logged-in person who opens `…/srv/wiki/` in a browser gets a search box to **ask** the wiki and read a cited answer, and can **follow links** from there into per-subject pages and onward through the subject graph. The web is **read-only navigation** — ask and browse, nothing that changes content — a styled human window onto the same `ask` and pages the MCP surface already exposes, with page and answer prose rendered as **formatted, sanitized markdown** rather than raw text; all mutating and operational work stays on MCP. Each ingest breaks text into the **subjects** it concerns and the **claims** it makes, then writes or updates each subject's page, so knowledge **compounds** — the LLM does the upkeep humans abandon; the user curates what goes in and asks. The owner can **merge** two names for the same thing into one page. Around that core the wiki lets the user steer the ingest queue (list, abort, re-run); reads are always served and never wait on the slow, strictly-serial ingest work. Every call site is configured **independently**. The wiki executes **no model calls itself**: all inference runs through the suite's central inference service (prompts), which also keeps the durable record of every call and its spend — wiki attributes each call to its stage, its job or question, and the person who caused it, so that record is inspectable and meaningful.

## Users

- **The owner, through an agent with the wiki's MCP.** Ingests topics mid-conversation, later asks questions answered from everything ingested, and merges two names for the same thing on spotting a duplicate. Never edits pages by hand.
- **The developer/operator, on the same MCP surface.** Sees inside — subjects, claims, pages, and job state — and acts: aborts a wedged job, re-runs one after fixing code or a prompt **off-line** (applied by restarting the service). For any inference call's exact request, response, and spend, they turn to the suite's central inference service, where wiki's calls arrive clearly attributed. First-class, not an afterthought.

## Scope

The wiki does this and only this:

- **Ingest text** — accept text, return a job handle immediately, integrate in the background. Each ingest infers the **subjects** and **claims**; raw text and claims are kept **permanently**.
- **Maintain per-subject pages** — write or update one coherent, deliberately **lossy** page per subject from its claims; a later mention updates the page rather than creating a duplicate.
- **Merge two subjects** — the owner names the **survivor** and the one to **fold in**; claims move to the survivor, its page is rewritten, the folded subject and page are removed, and the folded name thereafter **routes to the survivor** for ingest, `ask`, links, and direct `type/slug` lookup. Background (returns a handle), owner-approved only, no claim lost, **irreversible**. The owner can list merges performed, newest-first, paged.
- **Track and control jobs** — report each job as `pending`/`working`/`done`/`failed`/`aborted`; list newest-first filtered by **any combination of states** and/or a **time window**, paged; **count** matches without retrieving them; **abort** a pending/working job (nothing partial committed) into a distinct `aborted` state; **re-run** a terminal job from its original text, **replacing** earlier claims. Filterable states are discoverable; an invalid state is **refused with a clear error** naming the valid set; re-running a pending/working job is refused.
- **Attribute every inference call** — for extract, compile, ask, and the **embedding** calls that power search, hand execution to the suite's central inference service with the call labeled by its stage, correlated to its ingest job or its question, and attributed to the person whose action caused it (falling back to the service itself when no person did); each retry is its own labeled call. The durable record — requests, responses, tokens, spend — lives with that central service, filterable by those labels.
- **See ingest progress** — by handle, report a job's state and, when finished, the subjects it produced.
- **Ask** — find the wiki pages relevant to the question and synthesize a **cited** answer from **wiki content only** (the compiled pages, never raw claims or source text); best-effort and partial; when the wiki holds nothing on the question, answer that it holds nothing. The answer is **formatted markdown**, and the **first mention of each subject it names** is woven in as an **inline link** to that subject's page (the link text reads as the sentence's own words; the target is a fully-qualified front-door link), alongside the existing list of cited pages.
- **Inspect** — list subjects (filter by type and name, paged), view a subject's claims (paged), view its page.
- **Link pages** — each page shows the subjects it points to and those that point to it, both ways; a link exists when a subject's name — or a name **merged into it** — appears on another page, matched by exact normalized name (nothing fuzzy), shown by the survivor's name. In the page's own prose, the **first occurrence of each subject it points to** is also rendered as an **inline link** to that subject (its own name is never self-linked); the point-to/pointed-by summary stays in the footers.
- **Readable names** — every subject is named by its `type/slug` path (e.g. `entity/acme-corp`), accepted wherever a subject is requested; internal ids are never shown.
- **Page large results** — jobs, subjects, and claims return a bounded page plus a cursor; any filter narrows the set **before** paging.
- **Report liveness** — standard health, and a reflection that is **empty** (no event plane in this release).
- **Serve a read-only web surface** — at the mount root, present a small styled, session-gated web surface (for any signed-in dashboard human, like any web page in the suite; no valid session is turned away; agents continue on MCP) with three states, all consistent with the suite's shared visual style. Wherever compiled-page or answer prose is shown, it is rendered as **formatted markdown** — headings, emphasis, ordered and unordered lists, inline code and code blocks, blockquotes, GFM tables, and any inline links the prose itself contains — so a page reads like an article rather than showing raw `##`/`**` characters; the rendered output is styled with the suite's **shared design tokens** (the same `tokens.css` the rest of the suite uses — no new, ad-hoc styling system) and is **sanitized** so untrusted LLM-authored prose can never inject active HTML or scripts. **Home / reset**: a search box to ask the wiki, plus an index of the **subjects nothing links to** (those with no inbound link), so a subject no page mentions is still reachable; a small footer names the service and version. **Answer**: submitting the ask replaces the page with the cited answer — the same `ask` capability, drawn from compiled pages only — under an **"ask another question"** control that returns to the reset home, and a footer listing the **outbound** subjects (those whose name or alias appears in the answer prose), each navigable to its page. **Subject page**: reached by following a link, and addressable on its own, it shows the subject's compiled page prose under the same "ask another question" control, with a footer of **outbound** subjects (named in this page's prose) and **inbound** subjects (whose pages name this one). Linking follows the existing rule — a subject is linked when its name, or an alias merged into it, appears in the text by exact normalized match (nothing fuzzy), aliases routing to the survivor. Every referenced subject surfaces in the footers **and** the **first time it is named in the prose** it is an inline link to its page (on a subject page the page's own name is never self-linked); the prose also renders any markdown links it literally contains. **Every subject link — inline links and both footers, on the answer page and subject pages — is a fully-qualified front-door URL** (the same shareable, copy-paste-able form the `ask` MCP citations use), so a link lifted from any page resolves on its own; only the in-app navigation controls ("ask another question", the Home link) stay app-relative.
- **Stay responsive** — serve every read without waiting on an in-progress ingest, accept a new ingest immediately, integrate ingests **one at a time** (each visible all-at-once on completion), and let no slow or wedged job hold anything hostage (it can still be aborted).
- **Configure each call site independently** — `extract`, `compile`, and `ask` each get their own model, thinking level, and generation settings, with no two forced to match and no global; the **embedding** call site gets its own model and dimensions (one embedding model serves both page and query embedding, since the two must share a vector space); the configuration is inspectable.
- **Measure and tune the extract prompt at development time** — the extract prompt lives as a standalone file the running service carries with it, and the repo includes an offline workbench (never part of the deployed service) that scores that prompt — or any candidate — against a curated, committed, operator-reviewed gold corpus with **deterministic** scoring: the same candidate against the same corpus always gets the same score, and no model ever *judges* quality. The whole tuning run is **one command**: the developer names the pipeline step being tuned (this release: extract only) and specifies, per run, the exact call configuration — provider, model, and parameters — the prompt is measured under; the run tests **precisely that specification and nothing more** (no setting is inherited from committed files, and nothing the developer didn't specify is sent), so the workbench can answer "could this step run acceptably under *this* configuration?" before any decision to change the code. The command establishes the incumbent's baseline and noise floor under that configuration, then drives an **unattended improvement loop** that accepts a candidate only when it beats the incumbent beyond that noise. The run reports progress as it works, and **every accepted improvement is shown immediately as a diff against the committed prompt**; it keeps going until the developer's declared budget runs out, the score can't improve further, or the developer interrupts it — all ending the same way: a final winner confirmed on held-out cases the tuning never saw, with the evidence written down. Everything the run writes stays in uncommitted workspaces; a run that found no improvement says so and leaves the checkout clean. It runs on a plain local checkout with no suite services up. Adopting a winner is always a deliberate, manual change to the committed prompt file.

It deliberately does **nothing else** — in particular it does not: process more than one ingest at a time; touch the event plane (reflection is empty); **detect or propose** duplicates, or **merge or link** subjects by fuzzy/semantic name matching (merge, links, and direct `type/slug` lookup stay exact — an un-merged variant like "Gygax" for "Gary Gygax" resolves to nothing for those); **un-merge**; run background lint/dedup/contradiction/staleness maintenance; answer `ask` from raw claims or source text (it answers only from the compiled pages); structure pages differently per kind; draw a link across differently-named subjects by guessing; keep its own record of inference requests, responses, or spend (that record lives with the suite's central inference service); hold any model-provider credential or call any model provider directly (the running service, that is — the development-time workbench is not part of the service, adds nothing to it, and its existence changes no deployed behavior); judge prompt quality with an LLM (workbench scoring is deterministic); adopt an improved prompt automatically (adoption is always a deliberate manual change); allow interactive editing of a job, prompt, or page (changes are off-line, applied by restart); ingest anything but text; let `ask` write anything back; expose any mutating or operational action through the web surface (ingest, merge, and job control remain MCP-only); or introduce a bespoke styling system for the web surface (rendered markdown reuses the suite's shared design tokens, nothing new).

## Contractual constants

Promised values the design must honor verbatim and never re-declare:

- **Page size cap: 12,000 characters.** No subject page exceeds it — the cap forces a page to stay a compressed summary rather than an append-log.
- **Subject types are the closed set `entity`, `event`, `concept`.** Every subject carries exactly one.
- **A subject's public name is its `type/slug` path** (e.g. `entity/acme-corp`) — the only subject identifier ever shown or accepted; internal ids are never exposed.
- **The job lifecycle is the closed set `pending`, `working`, `done`, `failed`, `aborted`.** Every job is in exactly one; `aborted` (a deliberate cancellation) is distinct from `failed`; `done`/`failed`/`aborted` are terminal.
- **`ask` is strictly read-only — permanently.** It never creates or modifies wiki content (the accounting of its calls happens elsewhere and is bookkeeping, not a change).
- **The production LLM call sites are `extract`, `compile`, `ask`, and `embed`** — each named, each configured independently (the single `embed` model serves both page and query embedding, which must share a vector space).
- **Subjects match by normalized `type`-and-`name`** — the exact-match rule behind links, merges, and lookups.

## What we promise (user-facing behavior)

- **Ingest is fire-and-return, and the wiki stays responsive.** You get a handle immediately; throughout an ingest's multi-minute run every read returns promptly and new ingests are accepted. A stuck job holds nothing hostage — reads and submissions keep working and you can still abort it.
- **Ingests never corrupt each other.** Integrated one at a time, each completing before the next; a finished ingest's pages and claims appear **all together**, never half-applied.
- **Knowledge compounds.** Each subject gets one evolving page; feeding more text about it updates that page instead of piling up duplicates.
- **You can merge, but not un-merge.** Name which of two duplicate subjects survives and which folds in; afterward asking either name, ingesting under the old name, following a link to it, or looking it up by its old path all land on the one surviving page, with no claim lost. Merging is **irreversible** this release. You can list the merges you've made.
- **Jobs are siftable and steerable.** Watch one by handle; list newest-first filtered by any combination of states and/or a time window, paged; get a **count** cheaply; abort a queued or running job; re-run a finished/failed/aborted job as a clean do-over. A bad filter state fails with a clear error; re-running an unfinished job is refused.
- **Failures are legible.** A too-large or otherwise unusable ingest fails with a **clear, recorded reason** (not a cryptic parse error) and leaves nothing half-built.
- **Every inference call is on the suite's record, legibly.** Extract, compile, ask, and the embeddings that power search all run through the suite's central inference service, and each arrives labeled with its stage, tied to its ingest job or its question, and attributed to whoever caused it — so from that one place you can see what a job or a question asked, got back, retried, and spent, across re-runs. Wiki itself holds no provider keys and keeps no shadow copy.
- **The raw record is permanent;** pages are lossy, the source is not.
- **You can look inside and navigate.** List subjects, view any subject's claims and page, and follow a page's links (what it points to and what points to it) to related subjects — everything by readable `type/slug` name, never an internal id, a page at a time.
- **Ask works like an agent and is honest.** It finds the pages relevant to your question — by meaning and wording, not just exact names — and answers from them in **formatted markdown** that links the **first mention of each subject** inline to that subject's page (and lists each cited page too), every such link a fully-qualified front-door URL (so it opens whether the wiki runs in production or a local deployment, and you can copy it elsewhere); answers from those it has even if a related subject was never ingested; says "nothing here" plainly when the wiki holds nothing on the question; never fabricates from general knowledge; never changes anything.
- **Opening the wiki in a browser is a place you can use, not a placeholder.** A logged-in dashboard user who navigates to the mount root gets a small, on-brand page — styled consistently with the rest of the suite — with a search box to ask the wiki and a list of the subjects nothing links to, so every subject is reachable; someone without a valid session is turned away, and agents are unaffected — they keep working through MCP. A footer names the service and its version.
- **Ask on the web is the same ask as on MCP, and reads like an article.** Type a question and the page becomes the cited answer drawn only from compiled pages — or a plain "nothing here" when the wiki holds nothing — with an "ask another question" control above it that returns you to the empty home; the first mention of each subject in the answer is an inline link to its page, and the subjects the answer names are also gathered in a footer you can follow into their pages.
- **Every page is a node you can navigate from, and navigation changes nothing.** A subject page shows its compiled prose — with the first mention of each subject it points to rendered as an inline link (never a link to itself) — and a footer of the subjects it points to (named in its prose) and the subjects that point to it; following any of them lands on that subject's page, with the same "ask another question" escape back to home. The web only reads — it never alters a subject, claim, page, or job.
- **Web prose reads like an article, not source text.** On both the answer page and subject pages the compiled markdown is rendered with its formatting intact — headings, bold and italic, ordered and unordered lists, inline code and code blocks, blockquotes, GFM tables, the inline subject links (both the first-mention links the wiki weaves in and any the prose itself contains) — styled with the suite's shared design tokens so it looks like the rest of the suite, never a wall of raw `##` and `**`. The rendering is safe: any HTML or script woven into the LLM-authored prose is neutralized and can never run or alter the page.
- **Each call site runs its own model,** independently configured and inspectable — no single global model; the embedding model is configured the same way.
- **The extract prompt is measurable, and improvement is evidence-based.** From a plain local checkout — no suite services running — the developer scores the current extract prompt or any candidate against the committed gold corpus and gets a scorecard; the same inputs always score the same. A candidate is accepted only when it beats the incumbent by more than run-to-run noise, and a final winner is confirmed on held-out cases the tuning never saw. The claimed result can be re-verified: a winner's score reproduces from a clean checkout. The production prompt changes only when the developer deliberately changes the committed file — never as a side effect of tuning — and the deployed service is entirely unaffected by the workbench's existence.
- **Tuning is one command, watchable, and ends with a diff.** The developer launches the whole unattended run — baseline, then improvement loop — with a single command naming the step being tuned, and specifies **per run** the exact configuration the prompt is measured under — provider, model, and call parameters, with nothing inherited and nothing unrequested sent — and, separately, which model proposes candidates, all without editing any committed file. While it runs, scoring progress and each candidate's verdict are visible; the moment a candidate is accepted, the improvement appears as a diff against the committed prompt, and the final report ends with that diff plus the held-out confirmation. The run stops when its declared budget is exhausted, when the score is perfect, or when the developer interrupts it — and in every case it finishes the same way, evidence written, checkout clean. A run that never improved on the incumbent says exactly that and produces no diff.

## Success criteria (outcomes)

Each is a result the user can confirm against the running service:

- I ingest text and get a handle back without waiting for processing.
- During an ingest's multi-minute phase, job-status, jobs/subjects listing, claims, pages, and `ask` all return promptly, and I can submit another ingest immediately.
- A hung or long job blocks no read and no submission, stays abortable, and the service stays responsive throughout.
- A completed ingest's subjects and pages become visible all at once — never partially applied.
- I watch a job go pending/working → done (or failed/aborted) by handle, and list jobs newest-first filtered by any combination of states and/or a time window, paged; one query naming several states (e.g. all but `done`) returns them together.
- I get the **count** of jobs matching a state/time filter without retrieving them, correct across many jobs.
- Filtering by an invalid state yields a clear error naming the valid five; the valid states are discoverable from the jobs surface.
- I abort a pending or working job; it leaves nothing partial and shows as `aborted`, distinct from `failed`.
- I re-run a finished/failed/aborted job; it reprocesses from original text and **replaces** that attempt's claims; re-running a pending/working job is refused with a clear reason.
- Text too large to digest in one pass fails with a clear reason recorded on the job, leaving no partial subjects, claims, or pages.
- On the suite's central inference service I find every call wiki made — extract, compile, ask, and the page/query embeddings, each retry its own entry — labeled with its wiki stage; an ingest's calls all share that job's handle, one question's calls all share one correlation handle, and a call caused by my action is attributed to me.
- I page through jobs, subjects, and a subject's claims via cursor, with any filter applied before paging.
- After an ingest I list the subjects it produced, and for any subject view its claims and its page, referring to it by readable `type/slug` name.
- Every subject shown in a listing, page, or link is named by `type/slug`, and an `ask` citation comes back as a fully-qualified link to that subject's page (built from its `type/slug` and resolving through the front door in production or a local deployment); I never see an internal id.
- A page shows the subjects it points to and those that point to it, and I can follow any to that subject's page; a link exists exactly when that subject's name — or a name merged into it — appears on the page, never a merely-variant name.
- Ingesting more text about the same subject (same name) updates its page, not a duplicate.
- I merge two subjects by naming survivor and folded; afterward one page holds both subjects' claims, the folded subject and page are gone, and its claims persist relabelled on the survivor.
- After a merge, asking the folded name, ingesting under it, following a link to it, and looking it up by its old `type/slug` path all return the surviving subject — identical to the survivor's own path, not a not-found.
- A merge returns a handle promptly and completes in the background; nothing merges unless I request it.
- I cannot un-merge in this release.
- I list merges performed, newest-first, paged — what folded into what, and when.
- Original raw text and extracted claims remain retrievable after the page is built.
- I ask a question about a subject the wiki has — without needing to name it exactly, even using different words than the page uses — and get a cited answer synthesized from the relevant page(s), drawn only from ingested content.
- I ask a question spanning several subjects; the wiki answers from those it has and does not fail because one was never ingested.
- Asking something the wiki holds nothing on returns an explicit "nothing here," not a fabricated answer.
- Nothing I do through `ask` changes any subject, claim, or page.
- No page exceeds 12,000 characters.
- Health reports the service up; reflection reports no published or subscribed events.
- As a logged-in dashboard user I open the wiki's mount root and get a styled page with a search box and a footer naming the service and its version; without a valid session the page is refused, and the agent-facing MCP surface is unchanged.
- On the home page I see a list of the subjects that no page links to, and following one opens that subject's page; a subject some page does link to does not appear in that list.
- I type a question into the search box and the page becomes a cited answer built only from compiled pages — the same answer `ask` gives over MCP — and a question the wiki holds nothing on returns a plain "nothing here," never a fabrication.
- The answer page lists, in a footer, each subject whose name or alias appears in the answer, and following one opens that subject's page.
- In an `ask` answer (on MCP and on the web), the first time a subject is named the name is an inline link to that subject's page; naming it again later is left as plain text.
- On a subject page, the first time the prose names another subject that name is an inline link to it, and the subject's own name in its own prose is never linked.
- Every subject link the web surface shows — inline links and both footers, on the answer page and subject pages — is a fully-qualified front-door URL I can copy and open on its own; the "ask another question" and Home controls stay in-app links.
- An "ask another question" control on the answer page and on every subject page returns me to the empty home page.
- A subject page shows that subject's compiled prose and a footer with the subjects it points to (named in its prose) and the subjects that point to it; following any lands on that subject's page.
- On a subject page and on the answer page, markdown prose renders as styled HTML — a heading appears as a heading, `**bold**` as bold, list markers as a real list, fenced code as a code block, a blockquote as a quote, and a GFM table as a table — never as the literal `##`, `**`, `-`, or `|` characters.
- The rendered web prose is styled with the suite's shared design tokens (`tokens.css`), visually consistent with the rest of the suite — no separate styling system.
- An inline link present in the prose renders as a working link; prose containing no link renders no inline links.
- Raw HTML or a script embedded in compiled-page or answer prose is neutralized in the rendered page — it does not execute and cannot inject active markup.
- A subject named in the text by an alias merged into it links to the surviving subject's page, matched by exact normalized name — never a merely-similar name.
- Nothing I do on the web changes any subject, claim, page, or job; ingest, merge, and job control are not reachable from the web.
- I configure `extract`, `compile`, `ask`, and the `embed` model independently, and confirm which model runs behind each.
- Wiki holds no model-provider credential: with only the suite's central inference service reachable, every wiki capability that needs a model works.
- On a local checkout with no suite services running, I score the committed extract prompt against the gold corpus and get a scorecard with an overall score; scoring the same candidate against the same corpus again gives the same score.
- While a scoring run works, I see its per-case progress rather than silence.
- I score a candidate prompt file and get a plain accept-or-reject verdict against the incumbent that accounts for run-to-run noise — a within-noise wobble is a reject, not a win.
- I launch the entire tuning run — baseline plus unattended improvement loop — with one command naming the step, and I specify per run the full configuration the prompt is measured under — provider, model, and call parameters — plus the model proposing candidates, without editing any committed file; the run sends exactly the parameters I specified and nothing I didn't; naming a step the workbench doesn't support, or omitting the provider or model, fails with a clear error.
- Each time the loop accepts an improvement I see it immediately as a diff against the committed prompt; when the run ends with an improvement, the final output is that diff.
- The run stops when my declared budget runs out, when the score is perfect, or when I interrupt it — and in all three cases it still finishes: held-out confirmation, evidence, final diff.
- The improvement loop runs unattended, leaves the checkout clean (everything it wrote is in uncommitted workspaces), and reports its best candidate with the evidence; re-scoring that candidate from the clean checkout reproduces its claimed score.
- A run that never beat the incumbent tells me so plainly, produces no diff, and leaves nothing changed.
- A final winner is confirmed against held-out cases the tuning never touched, and a winner that fails there is reported as overfit rather than presented for adoption.
- The extract prompt production runs is the committed prompt file — editing that one file is the whole adoption step, and nothing the loop does ever edits it.
- The deployed wiki service is unchanged by the workbench: no new tool, no new endpoint, no provider credential, and no behavior difference attributable to the workbench's presence in the repo.
