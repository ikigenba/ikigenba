# Decision 5 ‚Äî The `state/` √∑ `cache/` backup boundary

**Decision.** Each service's on-box durable data is split by a single binary
boundary that does **triple duty**: it defines (a) what is backed up, (b) what is
wiped on restore, and (c) what the service must rebuild on boot.

- **`state/`** ‚Äî the **only** backed-up region. Holds the SQLite DB
  (`state/<svc>.db` + `-wal`/`-shm`) and any durable file space the service owns.
  This is the `/var/lib` of the service: "must survive."
- **everything else under `/opt/<svc>/`** that the service writes at runtime ‚Äî
  `cache/` (derived/rebuildable), `runs/`-style operational output, and the
  event-plane `cache/<svc>.db.generation` sidecar ‚Äî is the **non-state** region:
  the `/var/cache` + `/run` of the service, "may be deleted without data loss."
  Not backed up; reset on restore (D06).

The boundary is **explicit-include with one root**: a service backs up `state/`
and **nothing else** ‚Äî there is no per-service "extra root" mechanism. Durable
data is opted *in* by living under `state/`; the regular restore drills (D07) are
the mechanical enforcement that keeps the classification honest ‚Äî anything wrongly
left outside `state/` is discovered the first time a drill loses it.

**Served web content is ordinary `state/`.** Any service may have
`state/www/{public,private}` (D01); because it lives under `state/` it is backed
up and restored uniformly, with **no special-casing** ‚Äî `sites` is not special,
it is just the first service to use the pattern. The perms scheme that lets nginx
serve it without exposing the DB (the `0711` traverse-only `state/`, the `web`
group on `state/www/**`) is D01's; the backup boundary here treats `www/` as
plain `state/` content.

The **only** durable artifact handled outside this single root is the apex TLS
cert at `/etc/letsencrypt/` (a system path, not under any `/opt/<svc>/`), backed
up/restored by dashboard's dedicated cert stream (D07) for the rate-limit reason
recorded there.

This requires per-service relocation (D08): the DB moves to `state/<svc>.db` and
the sidecar to `cache/<svc>.db.generation`. Both paths are **composed in-binary
from the box-global `IKIGENBA_ROOT`** (D11) ‚Äî `${IKIGENBA_ROOT}/<svc>/state/<svc>.db`
and `${IKIGENBA_ROOT}/<svc>/cache/<svc>.db.generation` ‚Äî with explicit
`<APP>_DB_PATH` / `<APP>_GENERATION_PATH` overrides; no per-unit path stamping is
needed. Services with non-DB durable data (e.g. `prompts`' `sandboxes/`,
`scripts`' `runs/`) must consciously place what is durable under `state/` and
leave the rest outside ‚Äî that reclassification is the point of the model.

Because the snapshot is taken with the service **stopped** (D07), `state/` is a
consistent fileset at rest; the DB needs no `VACUUM INTO` / live-sync ‚Äî a plain
archive of `state/` (DB + `-wal`/`-shm` together) restores correctly.

**Rejected.**
- *Back up everything under `data/` minus an exclude list (fail-safe default).*
  Safer against silently dropping durable data, but risks archiving huge derived
  trees and, more importantly, lets "what is state" stay vague. The chosen
  include model + drills forces an explicit, exercised answer. (User decision.)
  Rejected.
- *Keep the DB outside the backed-up dir, `state/` = files only.* Splits "what is
  state" across two places and makes the backup collect two things; folding the
  DB into `state/` makes one dir the whole answer. Rejected.
- *Live snapshot (`VACUUM INTO` / `s3 sync`) with no stop.* Needed only if we
  refuse downtime; the suite's operating bet explicitly accepts short scheduled
  downtime, and stop-then-plain-tar is simpler and more obviously correct.
  Rejected.

**Verification.**
- R-46XM-U25R ‚Äî a backup archive of a populated service contains the `state/`
  contents (including `<svc>.db`) and contains **no** `cache/` entry and **no**
  `*.generation` entry. Substrate: real filesystem (temp dir) + the archive.
- R-485J-7TWG ‚Äî with the production env applied (`IKIGENBA_ROOT=/opt`, D11), the
  service's resolved DB path is under `state/` (`/opt/<svc>/state/<svc>.db`) and its
  resolved generation path is under `cache/` (`/opt/<svc>/cache/<svc>.db.generation`)
  ‚Äî the two land in their distinct tiers, not collapsed to `db+".generation"`.
  Substrate: appkit `config` unit test.
- R-49DF-LLN5 ‚Äî after `restore`, `state/` (DB bytes + every durable file) matches
  the snapshot that was restored. Substrate: real filesystem round-trip (and the
  real-S3 round-trip in D07).
- R-4ALB-ZDDU ‚Äî after `restore`, the non-state region reflects a clean slate: the
  pre-restore `cache/` contents and any stale `*.generation` are gone (not
  carried across from before the restore). Substrate: real filesystem round-trip.
